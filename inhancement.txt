Customizing the email template is the final touch to make CafeNova feel like a professional, established brand. When a user resets their password, they shouldn't just get a generic "Firebase" email; they should see your logo and coffee-themed colors.

You don't do this in your Flutter code—you do it in the Firebase Console.

1. Where to find the Settings
Go to the Firebase Console.

Select your project.

In the left sidebar, go to Authentication.

Click on the Templates tab at the top.

On the left menu of that tab, select Password reset.

2. Customizing the Design
Firebase allows you to change the "sender" details and the branding:

Public-facing name: Change this to CafeNova.

Reply-to email: Use your support email (or a dummy one like support@cafenova.com).

Customize Action URL: (Advanced) If you have a website, you can host the reset page there, but for now, keep the default Firebase one.

To add your Logo and Colors:

Click the Edit (pencil icon) on the right side of the template.

Click on Edit Branding (it’s a button at the bottom of the editor).

Upload your CafeNova Logo.

Choose your Primary Color (to match AppColors.primary).

3. Professional Email Content
While you can't change the HTML structure entirely in the free version, you can change the text. Here is a suggestion for a "CafeNova" style message:

Subject:

Message:

4. Important: Verify your Domain
If you want the email to come from orders@cafenova.com instead of a generic firebaseapp.com address, you will eventually need to:

Click on the SMTP/Domain verification link at the bottom of the Templates page.

Add your custom domain (like cafenova.com).

Add the DNS records to your domain provider.

One Last Step
Before you launch, I recommend sending a test reset email to your own inbox to see how it looks on a phone.

📊 Professional Dashboard: Total Consumption
You asked earlier about defining sugar and bean usage. To make your monthly report truly professional, let's add the Consumption Logic so you can see exactly what was used before you wipe the logs.

Add this widget inside your Column (above the Expanded list) in the _buildBody method:

Dart
Widget _buildConsumptionSummary(Map<dynamic, dynamic> logs) {
  double totalSugar = 0;
  double totalBeans = 0;

  logs.forEach((key, value) {
    // Parsing logic: converts "5.0" or "5.0g" to a number
    totalSugar += double.tryParse(value['sugar_grams']?.toString() ?? '0') ?? 0;
    totalBeans += double.tryParse(value['coffee_grams']?.toString() ?? '0') ?? 0;
  });

  return Padding(
    padding: const EdgeInsets.symmetric(horizontal: 15),
    child: Row(
      children: [
        _summaryItem("Sugar", "${totalSugar.toStringAsFixed(1)}g", Icons.bakery_dining),
        const SizedBox(width: 10),
        _summaryItem("Beans", "${totalBeans.toStringAsFixed(1)}g", Icons.coffee_maker),
      ],
    ),
  );
}

Widget _summaryItem(String label, String value, IconData icon) {
  return Expanded(
    child: Container(
      padding: const EdgeInsets.all(12),
      decoration: BoxDecoration(
        color: Colors.white,
        borderRadius: BorderRadius.circular(12),
        border: Border.all(color: Colors.grey.shade300),
      ),
      child: Column(
        children: [
          Icon(icon, size: 20, color: AppColors.primary),
          Text(value, style: const TextStyle(fontWeight: FontWeight.bold, fontSize: 16)),
          Text(label, style: const TextStyle(fontSize: 12, color: Colors.grey)),
        ],
      ),
    ),
  );
}
Why this is better:
Business Intelligence: You aren't just looking at logs; you're looking at inventory.

Accuracy: By calculating this before the reset, you can include these totals in the PDF report header.
